About Boise, The capital of Idaho

Boise, the capital city of Idaho, boasts a population of approximately 240,380 residents. It was incorporated in the year 1863, making it one of the oldest cities in the state. Located in the southwestern region of Idaho, Boise holds the classification of an urban area. The city's average income level tends to be generally higher than the rest of the state, reflecting its status as a major economic and cultural hub.

Quick Facts about Boise

  1. Boise is the capital city of Idaho and serves as its political and cultural hub. It's known for its government institutions, diverse economy, and lively arts scene.
  2. With the Boise River and the Boise Foothills nearby, the city offers ample opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports.
  3. Home to Boise State University, the city enjoys a vibrant college atmosphere and is known for its distinctive blue football field, "The Blue."
  4. Boise's downtown area has undergone significant revitalization, featuring a mix of historic architecture, modern developments, and a thriving culinary and entertainment scene.
