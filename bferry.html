About Bonners Ferry

Situated in the northern region of Idaho, Bonners Ferry is a charming city with a population of approximately 2,543 residents. It was officially incorporated as a city in 1899, contributing to its historical character. With a suburban and rural mix, Bonners Ferry offers a tranquil atmosphere for its residents. The city's average income level varies, but it often aligns with the state averages, reflecting the diversity of its economic landscape.

Quick Facts about Bonners Ferry

  1. Situated near the Canadian border, Bonners Ferry is known for its proximity to natural beauty and outdoor activities, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts.
  2. The city is located along the Kootenai River, offering opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and other water-based activities.
  3. Bonners Ferry preserves its history through landmarks like the Boundary County Museum, which showcases the area's pioneer heritage.
  4. Surrounded by picturesque landscapes, including the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains, Bonners Ferry provides stunning views and a tranquil environment.
